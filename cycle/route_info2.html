Discover Amsterdam

Amsterdam's Vondelpark is home to a selection of restaurants and cafés, including the Blauwe Theehuis, Café Vertigo, Vondelpark, De Vondeltuin and the Groot Melkhuis. You'll also find a skate rental shop, an open-air theater and a rose garden with more than 70 types of roses in the park. The Vondelpark was designed by landscape architect L.D. Zocher and has been awarded national heritage status.

'Vondelpark'

Vondelpark is the largest city park in Amsterdam, and the most famous park in the Netherlands, which welcomes about 10 million visitors every year. The Vondelpark is centrally located south of Leidseplein and near Rijksmuseum, Stedelijk Museum and Van Gogh Museum.

The Vondelpark is loved by people from Amsterdam, and is full of people - enjoying a sunny day, dog-walking, jogging, roller-skating, listening to music, people-watching, or just lazing about in grass.

Free concerts are given at the open-air theater or in the summer at the park's band stand. Vondel, the cast iron music dome, the Groot Melkhuis with playground for children, and the historical Pavilion with its restaurant Vertigo, opening in summer a popular terrace.
